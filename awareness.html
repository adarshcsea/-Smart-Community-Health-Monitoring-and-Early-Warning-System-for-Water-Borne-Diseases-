Cholera Infection Matrix

Acute intestinal toxigenic infection caused by the ingestion of food or water contaminated with the bacterium Vibrio cholerae. Accelerates via severe fluid losses.

Primary Diagnostic Signatures:
  • Profuse Watery Diarrhea
  • Rapid Muscle Cramping
  • Dehydration Spikes

Typhoid Systemic Fever

Systemic operational failure triggered by Salmonella enterica serovar Typhi ingestion. Spreads via poor sanitization infrastructure nodes.

Primary Diagnostic Signatures:
  • Sustained High Fever
  • Severe Migraine Axes
  • Abdominal Splenomegaly

Hepatitis A / E Virulence

Viral hepatic inflammation entering via oral-fecal routes. Leads to structural liver tissue strain and long clinical convalescence cycles.

Primary Diagnostic Signatures:
  • Icteric Sclera (Jaundice)
  • Dark Chromatic Urine
  • Severe Anorexia/Nausea
